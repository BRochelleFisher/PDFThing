INSTALLATION NOTES AND TESTING

  • Confirm the front wheels do not contact the mower, tractor, or other components.

    Your application may not look like these illustrations.

    • Test the front wheels through their entire steering range, including while the front axle is in its most extreme roll

      (oscillation) positions.

      • If needed, adjust the steering stops(1) (if applicable) and /

        or add spacers between the axle and frame to prevent front

        wheels from making contact.

    • Repositioning of the inner dishes(2) within the rims to reposition the front wheels in or out may be needed.

  • Tractors equipped with an axle stabilizer(3) must confirm the front axle oscillation does not allow more articulation then the cylinder

    stroke(4) allows.

    • Place the cylinder in float, and raise the right front axle up until

      it stops.

      • A SLIGHT amount of stroke(4) (silver portion of cylinder rod)

        should be visible; add an additonal cylinder stop or material

        to the right front axle as needed.

    • Repeat for the left side; the stroke(4) MUST be under 6”. add additonal material to the left front axle as needed.

  • After running the cylinders / mower / etc., the tractor and mower reservoirs will have been depleted some, and will need to be

    topped off.

    • Add hydraulic oil to the reservoirs as needed.

      (5)

    • Grease all grease zerks, pivot points, bearing surfaces, etc.

  • Use the separate supplied checklist to aid in your inspection.

    • Operate the attachment through full range of motion to confirm

      tractor stability on inclines, rough ground, etc.

  • Move the tractor to cleared ground and place the mower deck in its cutting position.

    • Flip the mower switch “ON”, with tractor at idle for 10 minutes,

      tractor at mid throttle for 10 minutes, and tractor at full

      throttle for 20 minutes.

      • Mower vibration should be smooth/minimal throughout the

        entire throttle range.

    • Flip the mower switch to “OFF”, and shut down the vehicle.

      • Once the mower has stopped, inspect all fittings /

connections for leaks, loose bolts, etc.

  • Adjust flow controls / constrictors for slower, smoother travel of mower and lift components.

  • OPTIONAL BRAKE FUNCTION: The mower should come to a complete stop in approximately 20-25 seconds.

    • If it stops quicker, adjust the allen cap screw(5) (clockwise to decrease time to stop, counterclockwise to increase

the time to stop).


Do not adjust the optional brake function to stop faster than 20 seconds, or severe damage can occur.

- Use an Allen wrench to remove the cap, and to adjust the cap screw(5) underneath.
